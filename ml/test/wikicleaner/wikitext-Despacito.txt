"'''Despacito'''" ({{IPA-es|despa'sito|am}}; "Slowly") is a song by Puerto Rican singer [[Luis Fonsi]] featuring Puerto Rican rapper and singer [[Daddy Yankee]] from Fonsi's 2019 studio album ''[[Vida (Luis Fonsi album)|Vida]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7647986/luis-fonsi-daddy-yankee-despacito-preview-exclusive|title=Watch an Exclusive Sneak Peek at the Making of Luis Fonsi & Daddy Yankee's New Single 'Despacito'|work=Billboard|last=Flores|first=Griselda|date=January 9, 2017|access-date=February 1, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8493018/luis-fonsi-new-album-vida-feb-1|title=Luis Fonsi Will Release New Album 'Vida' Feb. 1: Exclusive|work=Billboard|last=Cobo|first=Leila|date=January 10, 2019|access-date=January 10, 2019}}</ref> Released on January 12, 2017, the song was written by Fonsi, [[Erika Ender]] and Daddy Yankee, and produced by Mauricio Rengifo and [[Andrés Torres (producer)|Andrés Torres]]. A remix version featuring Canadian singer [[Justin Bieber]] was released on April 17, 2017, which helped to improve the chart performance of the song in numerous countries, including various number-one positions. "Despacito" has been widely credited by music journalists as being instrumental in popularizing Spanish-language pop music in the mainstream market again.

It is a [[reggaeton]] and [[Latin pop]] song composed in [[Time signature|common time]] with lyrics about desiring a sexual relationship, performed in a smooth and romantic way. "Despacito" received generally favorable reviews from music critics, who praised the fusion between Latin and urban rhythms, its catchiness, and its [[text painting]]. It has received [[Latin Grammy Awards]] for [[Latin Grammy Award for Record of the Year|Record of the Year]], [[Latin Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]], [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Urban Fusion/Performance|Best Urban Fusion/Performance]], and [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Short Form Music Video|Best Short Form Music Video]] at the [[18th Annual Latin Grammy Awards|18th Latin Grammy Awards]]. "Despacito" has been also ranked among the best Latin songs of all time and the best songs of 2017 by various publications, which referred to it as one of the most successful Spanish-language tracks in pop music history.

The song topped the charts of 47 countries and reached the top 10 of six others. In the United States, it became the first song primarily in [[Spanish language|Spanish]] to top the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] since [[Los del Río]]'s "[[Macarena (song)#English-language remix|Macarena]]" in 1996, subsequently tying the [[List of Billboard Hot 100 chart achievements and milestones#Most weeks at number one|longest-reigning number one on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100]] [[Old Town Road#Commercial performance|at the time]] with 16 weeks, as well as becoming the [[List of Billboard Hot Latin Songs chart achievements and milestones#Most weeks at number one|longest-running number-one on the Hot Latin Songs chart]] with 56 weeks. It also became the first Latin song to receive a [[Recording Industry Association of America certification#RIAA Diamond certifications|diamond]] certification by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]]. The music video shows both artists performing the song in [[La Perla, San Juan, Puerto Rico|La Perla]] neighborhood of [[Old San Juan]], [[Puerto Rico]] and local bar La Factoría. It was the [[List of most-viewed YouTube videos|most-viewed YouTube video]] of all time from August 2017 to November 2020 and became the first video on the site to reach the milestones of three, four, five, six, and seven billion views. It is now the second most-viewed video on the site, with "[[Baby Shark]]" by [[Pinkfong]] being the first most-viewed.

music.<ref name="enderbillboard" /><ref name="billboardint">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7661716/luis-fonsi-despacito-daddy-yankee-interview|title=Luis Fonsi Talks up mulling about "a song called 'Despacito'" and invited Brazilian-Panamanian singer and songwriter [[Erika Ender]] to a songwriting session.<ref name="ender october">{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/magazine-feature/7981961/despacito-latina-songwriter-erika-ender-interview|title='Despacito' Co-Writer Erika Ender Looks Back on 25 Years of Ups and Downs|work=Billboard|last=Cobo|first=Leila|date=October 3, 2017|access-date=October 17, 2017}}</ref> Fonsi co-wrote the song on a Gibson Emmylou Harris guitar.<ref>{{cite decided to give it an "urban injection" and sent a [[demo (music)|demo]] to reggaeton artist [[Daddy Yankee]].<ref name="billboardint" /> They recorded the song at Noisematch Studios in [[Miami, Florida|Miami]] in late 2016.<ref name="rollingstone">{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/daddy-yankee-talks-despacito-success-radiohead-meme-w481379|title=Daddy Yankee Talks 'Despacito' Success, Radiohead Meme|work=Rolling Stone|last=Spanos|first=Brittany|date=May 9, 2017|access-date=May 9, 2017}}</ref><ref name="noisematch" /> Daddy Yankee improvised his verse in a corner of the studio's [[control room]] while thinking about his father playing [[bongos]] and wrote the post-chorus.<ref name="noisematch">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jSOtSWvFCCg|title=Despacito Behind The Scenes: Alex J + Noisematch Studios|publisher=YouTube|date=August 27, 2017|access-date=March 17, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite 13, 2018}}</ref> It was produced by Mauricio Rengifo and [[Andrés Torres (producer)|Andrés Torres]]; the former is known as a member of Colombian pop duo [[Cali & El Dandee]] and the latter is known for previously working with [[David Bisbal]], [[Thalía]], and [[Ricky Martin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elpais.com.co/entretenimiento/despacito-se-escucha-hasta-en-china-esto-cuenta-luis-fonsi-de-su-gran-exito.html|title='Despacito' se escucha hasta en China, esto cuenta Luis Fonsi de [[Jaycen Joshua]] at [[Larrabee Sound Studios]] in [[North Hollywood, Los Angeles]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/photos/7997296/top-recording-studios-2017|title=Revealed: The Top Recording Studios of 2017|work=Billboard|date=October 11, 2017|access-date=March 18, 2019}}</ref>

Luis Fonsi does not consider it a reggaeton song but feels that "it does have a reggaeton energy and an subtle urban beat".<ref name="billboardint" /> Ender stated that the track "went through several arrangements" until Fonsi got "exactly the arrangement he wanted".<ref name="ender october" /> Fonsi described "Despacito" as a very melodic song that can adapt well to many other music genres.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7720915/luis-fonsi-despacito-hot-100-song-interview|title=Luis Fonsi Explains His 'Evolution of Sound' on Runaway Success 'Despacito'|work=Billboard|last=Havens|first=Lyndsey|date=March 13, 2017|access-date=March 14, 2017}}</ref> Due to the sensual nature of the song, they "needed to be responsible with a good lyric" and Ender's approach to writing for Fonsi was "to take care of how to say things with a good taste".<ref name="enderbillboard" /> In April 2017, American singer and songwriter [[Nicky Jam]] revealed that the original version of "Despacito" featured him instead of Daddy Yankee, but had to decline due to the song's release interfering with the launch of his album ''[[Fénix (Nicky Jam album)|Fénix]]''.<ref name="producers">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7865833/despacito-producers-interview-justin-bieber-remix|title='Despacito' Producers Talk Making a Smash Hit & Why Justin Bieber Should Be Respected|work=Billboard|last=Cobo|first=Leila|date=July 13, 2017|access-date=July 13, 2017}}</ref>

== Composition ==

of [[B minor]]<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Erika|first1=Ender|last2=Luis|first2=Fonsi|last3=Luis|first3=Ayala|last4=Yankee|first4=Luis Fonsi feat More|url=https://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/8063648/the-year-in-social-streaming-charts-bts-despacito-kendrick-lamar-more|magazine=Billboard|publisher=Billboard-Hollywood Reporter Media Mauricio Rengifo and Andrés Torres produced "Despacito" using [[Pro Tools]] and the final mix consisted of 47 tracks.<ref name="producers genius">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VMiLUt3tfuQ|title=The Making Of "Despacito" With Andrés Torres & Mauricio Rengifo|work=Genius|via=YouTube|date=July 14, 2017|access-date=July 14, 2017}}</ref> The song begins with a [[Puerto Rican cuatro]] played by [[Christian Nieves]], which is accompanied by an [[acoustic guitar]] played by Torres when Luis Fonsi starts performing.<ref name="producers genius" /> Rengifo stated that the guitar "was actually played, but then [they] chopped it and made it really digital."<ref name="producers genius" /> The producers decided to record a cuatro because they wanted "to feel very Puerto Rican and ethnic" and that it "gives the song a really unique character."<ref name="producers genius" /> Nieves played [[salsa music|salsa]]-influenced melodies during the chorus and the hook, which contains "old school pop" effects based on American producer [[Dr. Luke]].<ref name="producers genius" /> Percussion instruments guache and [[güira]] were synchronized with a [[Hi-hat|hi hat]] in order to highlight the track's [[cumbia music|cumbia]] influences.<ref name="producers genius" />

The song uses the [[Dynamic range compression|side-chaining]] production technique in order to make the chorus "more prominent," silencing the music as the [[kick drum]] hits.<ref name="medium">{{cite web|url=https://medium.com/that-good-you-need/weekly-billboard-theory-despacito-76359531a492|title=Weekly Billboard Theory – Despacito|publisher=Medium|last=Joffred|first=Robert|date=May 11, 2017|access-date=June 10, 2017}}</ref> It also makes heavy use of [[text painting]] when the music is slowed down as the word "despacito" (slowly) is performed at the beginning of every chorus.<ref name="medium" /> Its percussion consists of guache, [[Cowbell (instrument)|cowbell]], [[timbales]], güira, and sequenced [[drum]] patterns.<ref name="producers genius" /> The remix featuring Justin Bieber maintained the original rhythms and Luis Fonsi translated some lines to English, singing a verse in [[Spanglish]], while Daddy Yankee's verses were kept from the original version.<ref name="bieber" />

== Release and reception ==
"Despacito" was made available for digital download on January 13, 2017, by [[Universal Music Latin Entertainment|Universal Music Latin]]. Some music publications believed the single's success was influenced by a trend of combining [[Latin pop]] and urban music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.latercera.com/noticia/reggaeton-el-ritmo-que-se-quedo-para-siempre/|title=Reggaeton: El ritmo que se quedó para siempre|publisher=latercera.com|last=Fredes|first=Cristobal|date=August 13, 2016|language=es|access-date=April 15, 2017}}</ref> Fonsi referred to it as "the new pop" and Ender said that "everyone is making this type of fusions."<ref name="enderbillboard">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/events/latin-awards/7751668/erika-ender-on-co-writing-despacito|title=Erika Ender on Co-Writing 'Despacito': 'This Song Made a Special Connection'|work=Billboard|last=Fernandez|first=Suzette|date=April 5, 2017|access-date=April 17, 2017}}</ref> Luis Fonsi also stated that two weeks after the release of the song and its music video he started receiving calls from "people who normally don't call, people who only call when something different is going on." He got calls from Ricky Martin, [[Juan Luis Guerra]], [[Marc Anthony]] and other artists telling him that the track was a "home run."<ref name="q&a fonsi" />

Sebastian Wernke-Schmiesing of ''Dance-Charts'' journal stated that "a simple 4/4 time, [[Flamenco guitar|Spanish guitar]] sounds, a crisp bass, and the excellent vocals by Luis Fonsi and [Daddy Yankee] were enough to get a hit single from the start."<ref name="dancecharts">{{cite web|url=http://www.dance-charts.de/201704028481/musikvideo-luis-fonsi-feat-daddy-yankee-despacito|title=Musikvideo » Luis Fonsi feat. Daddy Yankee – Despacito|publisher=dance-charts.de|last=Wernke-Schmiesing|first=Sebastian|language=de|date=April 3, 2017|access-date=April 25, 2017}}</ref> [[Leila Cobo]] of ''Billboard'' praised its "undeniable immediate catchiness" and wrote that the song "is a clever blend of romantic Latin pop with a reggaeton beat and subtly naughty lyrics."<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7832978/despacito-myths-luis-fonsi-daddy-yankee-justin-bieber|title=Dispelling 5 Huge 'Despacito' Myths|work=Billboard|last=Cobo|first=Leila|date=June 14, 2017|access-date=June 21, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8053730/2018-grammy-nominations-analysis-despacito-gets-its-due|title=2018 Grammy Nominations Analysis: 'Despacito' Gets Its Grammy Due|work=Billboard|last=Cobo|first=Leila|date=November 28, 2017|access-date=December 2, 2017}}</ref> Robert Joffred of [[Medium (website)|Medium]]'s culture blog That Good You Need highlighted the use of a steel-string guitar to play [[flamenco]]-style melodies instead of a [[nylon-string guitar]], as well as the presence of "[[Swing (jazz performance style)|swung rhythms]]" when the word "Despacito" is sung at the beginning of the chorus<ref name="medium" /> He referred to the text painting as "pretty genius."<ref name="medium" /> Raisa Bruner of ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine described the single as "an infectious Latin melody amped up with reggaeton grooves and an irresistible dance tune."<ref name="time top" />

=== Synopsis ===
The video shows both artists performing the song while participating on different parties on the island, featuring model Zuleyka Rivera.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.latintimes.com/luis-fonsi-new-song-singer-teams-daddy-yankee-catchy-tune-despacito-video-409516|title=Luis Fonsi New Song: Singer Teams Up With Daddy Yankee For Catchy Tune 'Despacito'|publisher=latintimes.com|last=Wallace|first=Joan|date=January 13, 2017|access-date=February 1, 2017}}</ref> The clip starts with shots of La Perla's coast during daylight while showing Zuleyka Rivera arriving at the [[shanty town]] on foot. Luis Fonsi and Daddy Yankee perform the song in a street while elders are playing dominos, a child is getting his hair cut, two people are talking while listening to the radio, and couples are dancing in the background. The video intercalates with shots of Fonsi and Daddy Yankee singing in front of a car with people sitting on it and dancing next to it.
